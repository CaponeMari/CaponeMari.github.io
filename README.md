## **Comparing Crime Rates Bethlehem 2018 Infographic** 

![Infographic](https://github.com/CaponeMari/CaponeMari.github.io/blob/master/download%20(1).png?raw=true)

This data compares crime rates between Bethlehem, the state of Pennsylvania, and the country as a whole. The data set is a 2018 review of Bethlehem, PA crime found on AreaVibes, a website that evaluates the livability of different cities and towns. This data was released by the FBI in September of 2019, and maintained by AreaVibes as the most recent data regarding crime rates in Bethlehem. The page also included a crime map, different factors of data evaluating the safety of Bethlehem, and more visualizations and comparisons to nearby cities in PA. More information can be found here: [AreaVibes Bethlehem, PA Crime](https://www.areavibes.com/bethlehem-pa/crime/)

The key findings of this particular data set include how Bethlehem's overall crime rate is almost 1/4 below the national average. Additionally, Bethlehem's violent crime rate is over 1/4 below the national average. Compared to the US, Bethlehem's crime rates are not at all exceptional as none are above the national scale. However, types of crime including rape, burglary, theft, and property crimes were higher in Bethlehem than the state of Pennsylvania. Most notably, Bethlehem has a higher number of total crimes per 100k people than Pennsylvania as a state. In terms of Pennsylvania, Bethlehem has some above average crime rates, making the city seem more dangerous than alternative locations in PA. This is especially noticeable on the infographic because Pennsylvania has lower crime rates than the US in all but one category. Pennsylvania can be concluded safer in total crime ratings than the city of Bethlehem. Overall, the data implies that although Bethlehem may not be the safest city in PA, its crime rating is not significant on a nationwide scale.

## **TSA Checkpoint Travel Numbers 2020 and 2019 Comparison** 
The chart below displays TSA Checkpoint data from [TSA - An Official Website of the Department of Homeland Security](https://www.tsa.gov/coronavirus/passenger-throughput) that is updated daily. This specific chart represents biweekly data from March through October. The data compares travel throughput numbers in 2020 and 2019, emphasizing the impact of the Coronavirus pandemic on air travel. As anticipated, there is a significant disparity between travel numbers each year. The first points of travel numbers, occurring prior to the main outbreak of COVID-19, are very similar between the two years. From March to April of 2020, the travel numbers declined dramatically, experiencing an over 2 million traveler reduction. Since April, the 2020 travel numbers show a slow increase as the year progresses, but still remain well below the 2019 numbers. One interesting point is that the quick dips in the 2019 data points around May and September, most likely due to a less active travel time, do not reflect whatsoever in 2020. Instead, the data remains very steady throughout. This is most likely because the numbers are so much lower already, but it is notable that air travel has changed so much in the past year that it does not follow past trends in any way.

## **Top 10 Most Visited Cities in 2019**
Below is a map visualizing the top 10 most visited cities in 2019 created with [GoogleMyMaps](https://www.google.com/maps/d/). The cities are ranked by the amount of international visitors, with Bangkok, Thailand, ranked #1. The main attraction to Bangkok, among many of the other cities on the list, is the dense, expensive city atmosphere. These top cities are also ranked highly for the amount of money people spend there each day. Some other attractions to these destinations include iconic architecture and cultural settings, vacation spots, and the renowned "big name" of a city. Most cities on this list are appealing just by their distinguished name, but two less common cities were featured on the list as well. Ranked at #10 was Antalya, Turkey, a beautiful beach destination, and ranked at #6 was Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, containing the remarkable Pertonas Twin Towers. Cities including New York, Singapore, and Tokyo  accumulate many visitors regarding business. Additionally, cities like Paris, London, and Istanbul are popular for their cultural attitudes and notable landmarks such as Big Ben, the Eiffel Tower, and the many elaborate mosques in Istanbul. Each of these cities attracted over 12 million international travellers in 2019, a number that would have likey been exceeded in 2020 if the COVID-19 pandemic did not limit travel.
